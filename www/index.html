Dynazar
a DIY dynamic medium
based on Dynamicland
(but unaffiliated)
Dynazar is a browser-based clone of the 2017 iteration of Dynamicland. While that iteration is only a tiny step in a much grander vision, it is really cool by itself, too.
Dynazar is fun to play with and learn from. But if you have the chance, come visit Dynamicland in Oakland. Nothing beats the real deal!
There will be a set-up tutorial, but for now check out the Github repo, camera page, and projector page.
FAQ

Who created Dynazar? And why?
Hi, I'm JP. There are lots of reasons I could list for building Dynazar, such as having worked on interactive tools for many years, a background in programming education, having experimented with different representations of program execution. But the truth is, I was just unreasonably excited after trying Dynamicland for the first time, and just knew I wanted to try building something like that myself.

Much thanks to everyone who helped testing Dynazar. Special thanks to Omar Rizwan for sort-of instigating this project, and offering tons of ideas and feedback.

What are the differences between Dynazar and Dynamicland's implementation?
Well, I'm not a research group. I hacked this together in about two weeks, whereas Dynamicland has a full-time team who have worked for years on a new dynamic medium. As such, Dynazar is full of bugs, shortcuts, and other problems.

The most significant practical difference is the language. Dynamicland's operating system and programs are written in Realtalk, a newly developed dialect of Lua. Dynazar is written in Javascript, and runs in the browser, with OpenCV loaded via Webassembly.

Is Dynazar endorsed by Dynamicland?
No. While I know the Dynamicland team, I am not affiliated with them.

It's important not to misconstrue Dynazar as representative of the vision of Dynamicland. I got the sense that they are quite ready to move on from the specific implementation that I cloned in this project, as it has all sorts of issues. I can't wait to see their next iteration.

How can I help?

To really drive forward this research, please consider contributing to Dynamicland. They are looking for donations, volunteers, and partnerships.
